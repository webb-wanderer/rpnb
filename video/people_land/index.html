Don’t Die Stupid

People and The Land (1997)

People and The Land airdrops viewers into the universe of an occupied people, unreeling images of a new form of apartheid based on ethnicity. The documentary examines the concrete realities of Israel’s conduct in the West Bank and Gaza, and the filters and censorship through which Palestinian realities are represented in the U.S. media. It also emphasizes the direct connection between U.S. tax dollars and support of the Israeli military occupation, and the human cost of that aid in Palestine and the U.S.

Explosive moving picture footage rarely seen before. Suppressed by the mainstream media.

Duration 1 hour.
